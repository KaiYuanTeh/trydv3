LIFE EXPECTANCY

Life expectancy is a statistical measure of the average number of years a person
can expect to live.

Life expectancy is influenced by a number of factors including access to healthcare,
education, genetics, nutrition, sanitation, choice of lifestyle and et cetera.

The chosen dataset for this visualisation was collected from the Global Health
Observatory (GHO) data respository under the World Health Organization (WHO).

The dataset collected contains data from year 2000 to 2015 for 193 countries around the
world.

AVERAGE LIFE EXPECTANCY FOR SIX CONTINENETS ACROSS THE WORLD
FROM 2000 TO 2015

By looking at the graph below, we can tell that the continent South America has the highest
value of average life expectancy - 77.65 while the continent Africa has the lowest value of
average life expectancy - 58.79 among all other continents over the 16 years.

The difference of the average life expectancy value between South America and Africa is
18.86 which is a significant gap.

COUNTRIES WITH TOP 5 HIGHEST OR LOWEST VALUE OF AVERAGE LIFE EXPECTANCY
ACROSS THE WORLD FROM 2000 TO 2015

From the graph, we can see that the top 3 countries with the highest average life expectancy value are
Japan - 82.54, followed by Sweden - 82.52 and Iceland - 82.44.

From the graph, we can see that the top 3 countries with the lowest average life expectancy value are
Sierra Leone - 46.11, followed by Central Africa Republic - 48.51 and Lesotho - 48.78.


RANKING AMONG THE TOP 10 COUNTRIES WITH THE HIGHEST AVERAGE
LIFE EXPECTANCY OVER YEARS

We can observe that Japan has consistently ranked number 1 in terms of average life
expectancy for many years, especially the year 2012, 2013, 2014 and 2015 which make it hit
a four in a row straight for ranked number 1.

Next, we can see that Norway have seen significant improvements in life expectancy over
the year 2002 to 2009 as it ranked number 10 in year 2002 and ranked number 1 in year
2009.

ADULT MORTALITY RATE IN DIFFERENT COUNTRIES ACROSS THE WORLD FROM
2000 TO 2015

We can see that the adult mortality rates have decreased in most countries over the time.

In the year 2000, Portugal and Saudi Arabia have the lowest adult mortality rate - 12 and Zimbabwe
has the highest adult mortality rate - 665.

In the year 2015, United States of Amerca has the lowest adult mortality rate - 13 and Lesotho has
the highest adult mortality rate - 484.

GROSS DOMESTIC PRODUCT (GDP) GROWTH OVER TIME OF TOP 10
DEVELOPED COUNTRIES

From the graph, we can conclude that the value of GDP increased over the time for most
of the countries.

We can see that the GDP value of Switzerland has been growing consistently over the
time. However, the waterfall chart shows a sudden drop in GDP growth in the year 2003,
we can conclude that Switzerland experienced a recession in that year. But we can also
see that there is a sudden increase in GDP value in year 2004 which means Switzerland
experienced a period of economic expansion that year and has recovered from the
previous recession.

HEPATITIS B IMMUNIZATION COVERAGE (%) OF DIFFERENT COUNTRIES ACROSS
TIME

The graph showed that the overall trend of the Hepatitis B immunization coverage increased over
the time stablely.

In the year 2000, Lesotho has the lowest Hepatitis B immunization coverage rate - 9% and Australia
has the highest Hepatitis B immunization coverage rate - 94%.

In the year 2015, Chad has the lowest Hepatitis B immunization coverage rate - 46% and Spain has
the highest Hepatitis B immunization coverage rate - 97%.


TOTAL EXPENDITURE PERCENTAGE (%) SPENT ON THE HEALTH SECTORS
BY GOVERNMENT OF DIFFERENT COUNTRIES

The overall trend in total expenditure percentage is remaining reliatively stable in most
countries.

Japan has the greatest magnitude of change in total expenditure percentage between
year 2000 and 2014 which is 6.31% and Central African Republic has the smallest
magnitude change in total expenditure percentage between year 2000 and 2014
which is 0.04%.

In the year 2000, Sierra Leone has the highest total expenditure percentage - 13.63%
while Angola has the lowest total expenditure percentage - 2.79%. In the year 2014,
Sweden has the highest total expenditure percentage - 11.93% and Japan has the
lowest total expenditure percentage - 1.23%.

BODY MASS INDEX (BMI) AGAINST AVERAGE SCHOOLING DURATION IN YEARS
AMONG DIFFERENT CONTINENTS

By looking into the graph, there is a strong positive relationship between BMI and schooling years
for all continents.

Australia located in Oceania has the highest average schooling years value which is 58.2 while
East Timor located in Asia and Federated States of Micronesia located in Africa have the lowest
average schooling years value which is 0.

Kiribati located in Oceania has the highest average BMI value - 67.9 and Bangladesh has the
lowest average BMI value - 1.4.
